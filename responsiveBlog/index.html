What is an Article?

Articles—a, an, and the—are placed before nouns to specify whether the noun is being referred to in a general or specific way. Indefinite Articles: “A” and “An” are indefinite articles used to refer to something non-specific. Example: I saw a cat. (refers to any cat, not a particular one) Example: She is an engineer. (“an” is used because “engineer” starts with a vowel sound) Definite Article: “The” is used when talking about something specific or known to both the speaker and listener.
